汉字 - Han Zi - 漢字

三百千 - Three, Hundred, Thousand

三百千 (San Bai Qian, Three Hundred Thousand) are three texts used as introductory literacy texts after the Song Dynasty. Together, they contain about 2,000 characters, which is a good foundation for learning the other 13,000+ hanzi.

  • 三字經 (San Zi Jing, Three Character Classic) is a summary of Confucianism suitable for teaching young children.
  • 百家姓 (Bai Jia Xing, Hundred Family Surnames) is a list of around 500 surnames circa the Song Dynasty, in no particular order.
  • 千字文 (Qiān Zì Wén, Thousand Character Classic) is a poem containing exactly one thousand characters, each used only once. Its structure and content make it akin to an "alphabet song".

部首 - Radicals

Radicals or "section headers" are parts of hanzi used to classify and group hanzi. They may be semantic components, phonetic components, or just an isolated section of a character. Many are derived from the simplest hanzi.

I decided to try using those radicals which are also hanzi in their own right as a memorization scheme that can hopefully give me a boost in identifying and memorizing more complex hanzi.
